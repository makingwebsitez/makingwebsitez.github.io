A Critical Analysis of Hannibal (the Show and Character)

Introduction

The television series Hannibal, while stylish and visually appealing, presents a titular character whose intelligence, physical abilities, and overall narrative enjoyability leave much to be desired. In this critique, we will delve into the shortcomings of both the show and its central character, Hannibal Lecter.

Critique of Hannibal's Intelligence

  • Hannibal's so-called "genius" often hinges on the incompetence of other characters rather than his own ingenuity.
  • His manipulations are contrived and rely heavily on improbable coincidences.
  • The show tries to portray him as a mastermind, but many of his plans lack logical coherence and fall apart under scrutiny.

Critique of Hannibal's Physical Abilities

  • Despite his refined demeanor, Hannibal's physical prowess is highly exaggerated and unrealistic.
  • Fight scenes involving Hannibal often defy believability, portraying him as unnaturally invincible.
  • His supposed combat skills lack consistency and fail to align with his character's established traits.

Critique of Hannibal's Fighting Skills

Hannibal's fighting abilities are often depicted as superior, but this portrayal feels forced. His victories depend on convenient circumstances rather than skill:

  • Opponents are frequently written as inept, diminishing the credibility of his victories.
  • The choreography is more about style than substance, undermining the realism of his combat scenes.

Critique of the Writing

The writing of Hannibal's character is inconsistent and overly indulgent:

  • His dialogues are often pretentious and lack genuine depth.
  • The show focuses more on aesthetics than substance, leading to shallow character development.
  • Hannibal's actions frequently contradict his supposed intellect and refined persona.

Enjoyability of Hannibal's Character

While some viewers may find Hannibal's character intriguing, his lack of relatability and the show's overemphasis on his villainous charm make him less enjoyable:

  • The character's cold detachment alienates the audience rather than engaging them.
  • His motivations are often unclear, leaving viewers confused rather than captivated.
  • Over-reliance on shock value detracts from meaningful storytelling.

Conclusion

In conclusion, Hannibal Lecter, as portrayed in the show Hannibal, is an overhyped character whose intelligence, physical abilities, and narrative appeal fall short of expectations. While the show has its merits, its central figure leaves much room for critique and improvement.
